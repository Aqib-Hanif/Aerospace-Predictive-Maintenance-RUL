# Aerospace Predictive Maintenance using Deep Learning

## Domain of Interest
Aerospace Engineering + Artificial Intelligence  
Focus: Predictive Maintenance and Remaining Useful Life (RUL) estimation

## Research Motivation
Traditional aircraft maintenance is time-based and costly. 
Modern predictive maintenance uses sensor data and AI models to predict component failure before it occurs.

However, most existing deep learning models focus only on improving prediction accuracy and ignore:

- Uncertainty estimation
- Model explainability
- Risk-aware decision support

In aerospace systems, safety and reliability are critical. Therefore, uncertainty-aware and explainable AI models are necessary.

## Proposed Research Idea
Title (Tentative):

Uncertainty-Aware Attention-Based LSTM Model for Turbofan Engine Remaining Useful Life Prediction

## Core Idea
- Use NASA C-MAPSS turbofan dataset
- Develop an LSTM-Attention model
- Integrate Monte Carlo Dropout for uncertainty estimation
- Compare deterministic vs probabilistic predictions
- Evaluate RMSE, MAE, PHM score, and calibration

## Expected Contribution
1. Improve reliability of RUL prediction
2. Provide confidence intervals for maintenance decisions
3. Enhance trust in AI-driven predictive maintenance

## Dataset
NASA C-MAPSS Turbofan Engine Degradation Dataset
